Hermitcraft Season 8 Economy

Introduction

This Project is made in colloboration with these trackers, who each recorded the various transactions of Hermitcraft members. We were only interested in transactions that were made with diamonds. All episodes from each hermit were checked and we incorporated as many livestreams as we could.

Who is trading with who?

This chord diagram represents the flow of diamonds between hermits.

You can hover over a hermit's name to see all transactions in and out or hover over an individual ribbon to see how many diamonds transferred between 2 specific hermits during Season 8.

Selecting a hermit in the dropdown will highlight transactions between them and other hermits without having to hover.

Who is trading with who 2?

This Sankey diagram is another way of representing the flow of diamonds between hermits.

You can find your favourite hermit and trace the lines from the Payers (The ones who spend the diamonds) to the Payee (The ones who receive the diamonds.) This way you can easily track how many diamonds were traded between 2 hermits over the whole season indicated by the thickness of the ribbon connecitng them, as well as a really good indication of how much a hermit spent and received in total over the season indicated by the height of their bars on the left and right.

Etho, aka Ladders, with Iskall's wallet purchased just about everything that caught his eye, can't have too much right?
And Finally Doc, big ambitions means you need a big wallet right? Despite a trade war, Doc conducted much of his business with Jevin buying beacons and sponges a plenty.

The battle of the shopping districts.

Shopping districts quickly became a central part of this season, but how well do they compare? Boatem takes a very heavy lead in front of any others with the economic powerhouse of the G-Train pushing them forward, a healthily diverse set of shops also sets them apart.
Horse Head Farms while a bit of sleeper were able to generate massive swathes of revenue through their IOU auction.
The Evil Emporium primarily benefited from the Diamond to Derpcoin exchange
Big Eyes were able to make a healthy profit through forcing Keralis to work away underground trading villagers for XP bottles so Mumbo would have XP at his disposal depsite having an "XP farm".
Finally Octogan, don't let the diamonds fool you, Ren and Doc raked in with the derpcoin, forming a healthy reserve but unfortunately those are not tracked in our project.

Spending vs Earning

We can see here a comparision between how many diamonds each Hermit spends and how many they make back in their shops. Some Hermits hold very tightly to their riches, rarely spending which can act as an inflation control measure. Whereas others are equally keen to dump money back into the economy as they were to profit from it, with Pearl, Scar, Impulse, Tango, xB and Gem all having roughly equal spending and earning whereas Cub and Etho far outspend their shops' revenue generation.

Interesting Facts!

  • The hermits spent on average 232 Diamonds this season, with average of 14 Diamonds spent per transaction
  • The hermits' shops garnered an average of 123 Diamonds in revenue but a median of 34.
  • September was the month with the most Diamonds sp ent, with 1666.
  • June saw 160 Diamonds spent, July had 1035, August 1018, September 1666, October 1312, November 672 and December only had 168 diamonds spent.
  • The hermit with the most total transactions was Cub with 72
  • The total number of Diamonds spent was 6033
